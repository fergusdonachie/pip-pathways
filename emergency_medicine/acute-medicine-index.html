Evidence summary (what this index is for)

Key design points
  • In primary care, “acute medicine” work is mostly recognise (time-critical patterns), measure (full obs), and route (same day) — rather than definitive diagnosis.
  • For most acute pathways, the highest-yield items to document before sending are: NEWS2 (if used locally), SpO2 on air, RR, BP, HR, Temp, mental state, and urine output (when relevant).
  • Sepsis guidance has been split by population. For adults ≥16 (not pregnant/recently pregnant), NICE NG253 provides the core risk stratification (community table) and early escalation principles.

Current included drafts

  • Suspected sepsis (age ≥16, not pregnant) — recognise high-risk criteria in the community and route for urgent ED/SDEC assessment; don’t rely on fever.
  • Lead poisoning / lead exposure — suspected exposure (children/pregnancy/adults): when to test, what to do with results, and who to involve (public health / occupational health / NPIS).

Planned next (not drafted yet)

  • Acute kidney injury (recognition + same-day bloods + escalation)
  • Delirium / acute confusion (sepsis/metabolic/SAH red flags + safeguarding)
  • Acute abdomen red flags (peritonism, AAA pattern, obstruction)

Acute medicine — specialty index (primary care)

Use this page when

  • You want quick links to acute-med triage pathways, and a reminder of the common handover minimum (observations + key “sick” markers).

Pathways

  • Suspected sepsis (≥16, not pregnant) — community triage
  • Lead poisoning / lead exposure (suspected)

General handover / referral minimum (acute medicine)

  • Core obs: HR, BP, RR, Temp, SpO2 on air (and whether on oxygen), AVPU/GCS, capillary glucose.
  • NEWS2 (if used) and single worst parameter(s).
  • Hydration/perfusion: urine output (if known), CRT, peripheral temperature/mottling.
  • Working differential: suspected source (chest/urine/abdo/skin) and key negatives.
  • PMH: frailty, CKD, heart failure, immunosuppression, recent procedures, indwelling devices.
  • Meds/allergies: antibiotics in last 3 months; steroid/immunosuppressants; anticoagulants.
  • Treatment already given: fluids/antibiotics/oxygen/antipyretics.
  • Social: ability to cope at home; safeguarding concerns; baseline cognition/function.
