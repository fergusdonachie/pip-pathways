DGRefHelp drafts / Suspected TIA / minor stroke

Acute focal neurological symptoms: treat any ongoing deficit as stroke (999/ED). If symptoms have resolved (suspected TIA), start aspirin 300 mg (unless contraindicated) and arrange specialist assessment within 24 hours (do not use ABCD2 to triage urgency).
Mode: quick
Last updated: 2026‑02‑11

Evidence summary (triangulated)

Key design points (primary care)
  • Ongoing symptoms = stroke until proven otherwise: arrange emergency transfer (999/ED) for acute focal neurological deficit that is current or fluctuating.
  • For suspected TIA (symptoms fully resolved), NICE NG128 recommends:
    • Start aspirin 300 mg immediately (unless contraindicated).
    • Refer immediately for specialist assessment/investigation, to be seen within 24 hours of symptom onset.
    • Do not use ABCD2 (or similar scores) to determine urgency.
  • NICE NG128 also advises: do not offer CT brain scan for suspected TIA unless an alternative diagnosis is suspected that CT could detect; MRI is typically considered after specialist assessment.

Suspected TIA / minor stroke — initial actions (primary care)

Immediate triage

  • Ongoing / fluctuating / progressive focal deficit = treat as suspected stroke → 999 / ED now (even if “mild”).
  • Symptoms fully resolved = suspected TIA (by definition within 24 hours) → act urgently (below).
  • Use a validated tool such as FAST to screen for stroke/TIA symptoms in the community (NICE NG128).

Emergency (treat as stroke / emergency assessment)

  • Any ongoing focal deficit: face/arm/leg weakness, speech disturbance, visual loss/field defect, neglect, ataxia.
  • Reduced consciousness, seizure at onset, severe/worsening headache, meningism/fever.
  • Posterior circulation features with ongoing symptoms: new severe vertigo/ataxia, diplopia, dysarthria, dysphagia.
  • On anticoagulation with acute neuro symptoms → emergency assessment.

Suspected TIA: what to do now (do not delay referral)

  • Exclude hypoglycaemia (capillary glucose) as a mimic (NICE NG128).
  • Give aspirin 300 mg immediately (then daily) unless contraindicated (NICE NG128).
  • Refer immediately for specialist assessment/investigation, to be seen within 24 hours of symptom onset (NICE NG128).
  • Do not use ABCD2 (or similar scores) to decide urgency (NICE NG128).

Assessment / referral information (keep tight)

  • Time matters: time last known well; onset time; duration; time symptoms fully resolved; current status (back to baseline vs residual).
  • Symptoms: speech/language, face/arm/leg weakness, sensory loss, visual loss/field defect, ataxia/vertigo, dysphagia; negative vs positive symptoms (mimics) — but don’t delay pathway if uncertain.
  • Obs + exam: HR, BP, RR, Temp, SpO2; focused neuro exam findings (even if normal now).
  • Risk/PMH: AF/arrhythmia, prior stroke/TIA, carotid disease, diabetes, smoking, hypertension, hyperlipidaemia.

Investigations in primary care (only if they do not delay the 24h assessment)

  • 12‑lead ECG (look for AF/arrhythmia) if available without delaying referral.
  • If easily available without delaying: FBC, U&Es/eGFR, glucose/HbA1c, lipids.

Imaging (primary care)

  • Do not arrange CT brain routinely for suspected TIA unless an alternative diagnosis is suspected that CT could detect (NICE NG128).
  • Carotid imaging/MRI are typically decided after specialist assessment.

Who to refer / where to send (D&G placeholders)

  • 999 / ED: any ongoing or fluctuating deficit; reduced consciousness; seizure at onset; severe headache/meningism; on anticoagulation with acute neuro symptoms.
  • Urgent TIA / neurovascular assessment (within 24h): suspected TIA (symptoms resolved) — refer immediately via local TIA/stroke pathway.
  • Advice & guidance: diagnostic uncertainty in a now‑well patient, or complex antithrombotic questions (e.g. already anticoagulated).

Referral‑letter minimum (suggested)

  • Onset/offset times; symptom description; current status.
  • Obs + neuro exam; capillary glucose.
  • ECG result if done; relevant bloods if already available.
  • Medications: antiplatelets/anticoagulants; statin; antihypertensives.

Local agreement needed (add D&G specifics)

  • Local route for suspected TIA within 24 hours: phone hotline vs eRS urgent clinic vs direct admission/SDEC vs ED (and out‑of‑hours plan).
  • Local expectation for ECG/bloods before transfer vs done after arrival (do not delay referral).
  • Local policy for aspirin 300 mg when already on an antiplatelet/anticoagulant (continue current agent vs add aspirin vs specialist advice).
  • Local criteria for same‑day carotid imaging (if any) and who orders it.
  • Local patient info leaflet / wording (“if symptoms recur → 999”).
Safety net
If neurological symptoms recur or do not fully resolve, treat as suspected stroke and arrange emergency assessment (999).
