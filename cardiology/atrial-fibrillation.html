DGRefHelp drafts / Atrial fibrillation (new diagnosis + anticoagulation)

Adults with newly diagnosed AF: confirm on 12‑lead ECG, assess stability, reduce stroke risk using CHA2DS2‑VASc + bleeding risk (ORBIT), and initiate DOACs where locally agreed.
Mode: quick
Last updated: 2026‑02‑10

Evidence summary (triangulated)

Key design points (primary care)
  • For suspected AF: confirm with a 12‑lead ECG if an irregular pulse is detected (NICE NG196).
  • Stroke prevention decisions should use CHA2DS2‑VASc for stroke risk assessment (NICE NG196).
  • Bleeding risk: NICE NG196 recommends ORBIT (higher accuracy for absolute bleeding risk prediction than other tools). Use it to support shared decision making and to drive risk factor modification (e.g. uncontrolled HTN; interacting meds; alcohol; anaemia).
  • Anticoagulation thresholds (NICE NG196):
    • Offer anticoagulation (DOAC) if CHA2DS2‑VASc ≥2.
    • Consider anticoagulation (DOAC) for men with CHA2DS2‑VASc = 1.
    • Do not anticoagulate people <65 with no risk factors other than sex (CHA2DS2‑VASc 0 for men or 1 for women).
    • Do not withhold anticoagulation solely because of age or falls risk.
  • NICE NG196 recommends DOACs first line for eligible people. If DOACs are contraindicated/not suitable, offer a vitamin K antagonist.

Atrial fibrillation — new diagnosis + anticoagulation (adults)

Immediate triage (999 / ED now)

  • Haemodynamic instability: hypotension/shock, acute pulmonary oedema, ongoing syncope, chest pain suggestive of ACS, new severe breathlessness/hypoxia.
  • Very fast ventricular rate with concerning symptoms (use local thresholds) OR suspected ventricular tachycardia / broad complex tachycardia.
  • Stroke/TIA symptoms → emergency stroke pathway.

Confirm diagnosis

  • If an irregular pulse is detected and AF suspected: perform a 12‑lead ECG to diagnose AF (NICE NG196).
  • If AF suspected but not seen and episodes are intermittent: arrange ambulatory ECG monitoring chosen by symptom frequency (see Palpitations page / NICE NG196).

Initial assessment (aim: stability + reversible contributors + baseline for safe anticoagulation)

  • Observations: HR, BP, RR, Temp, SpO2 on air.
  • History: onset/time of onset if known; symptoms (chest pain, breathlessness, presyncope/syncope); stroke/TIA history; heart failure/valve disease; bleeding history; alcohol; interacting medicines (NSAIDs, antiplatelets, SSRIs).
  • Examination: heart failure signs; murmurs (valvular disease); volume status.
  • Baseline tests (typical): FBC (anaemia/platelets), U&Es/eGFR, LFTs, TFTs; glucose/HbA1c/lipids per cardiovascular risk work; consider magnesium if acutely unwell.
  • Echocardiography: do TTE if important for long-term management, rhythm-control strategy, or suspected structural heart disease (NICE NG196). Do not do TTE solely to further stroke-risk stratify if anticoag decision already agreed (NICE NG196).

Stroke risk assessment (CHA2DS2‑VASc)

  • Use CHA2DS2‑VASc for stroke risk in AF/atrial flutter/paroxysmal/persistent/permanent, including post-cardioversion/ablation recurrence risk (NICE NG196).
  • Document the components in the record and referral letter (so it’s auditable).

Bleeding risk assessment (ORBIT) + modify risk

  • Assess bleeding risk when starting or reviewing anticoagulation using ORBIT (NICE NG196).
  • Actively modify bleeding risks where possible (NICE NG196), e.g.:
    • Uncontrolled hypertension.
    • Interacting drugs (antiplatelets, NSAIDs, SSRIs) — review necessity.
    • Alcohol (harmful use) — address.
    • Anaemia — investigate reversible causes.

Anticoagulation decision (NICE NG196)

  • Offer anticoagulation with a DOAC if CHA2DS2‑VASc ≥2, taking bleeding risk into account.
  • Consider anticoagulation with a DOAC for men with CHA2DS2‑VASc = 1, taking bleeding risk into account.
  • Do not offer anticoagulation for people <65 with no risk factors other than sex (CHA2DS2‑VASc 0 men / 1 women).
  • Do not withhold anticoagulation solely because of age or falls risk.
  • If DOACs are contraindicated/not tolerated/not suitable: offer a vitamin K antagonist (NICE NG196).
Practical safety notes (non-prescriptive)
For DOAC prescribing, ensure you have an up-to-date weight and renal function, check for interacting medicines, and choose the correct dose (renal impairment/age/weight criteria differ by agent). Use local medicines guidance/BNF/MHRA advice.
