Evidence summary (what this index is for)

Key design points
  • Mental health presentations become high-risk when immediate safety (suicide, self-harm, violence, safeguarding) or capacity are in question.
  • NICE self-harm guidance (NG225) emphasises: do not use risk scores to predict suicide/repetition or to decide discharge; focus on needs and safety and ensure timely access to psychosocial assessment.
  • Many pathways are local-process sensitive: crisis routes (CMHT/crisis team), out-of-hours arrangements, and local safeguarding workflows.

Mental health — specialty index (primary care)

Pathways

  • Self-harm / suicidal ideation — immediate triage + referral

General referral letter / triage metrics (mental health)

  • Risk context: current suicidal thoughts/intent, plan, means, access to means, recent self-harm, escalation, protective factors, supervision/support.
  • Mental state: mood/anxiety, psychotic symptoms, intoxication/withdrawal, agitation, hopelessness.
  • Capacity/consent: ability to engage, consent to contact family/carers; safeguarding concerns.
  • Physical safety: injuries/poisoning, obs, red flags for urgent physical assessment.
  • Past history: previous self-harm/suicide attempts, MH diagnoses, current MH team involvement.
  • Medication: current psych meds; recent changes; quantities at home; high-toxicity meds (e.g., TCAs).
  • Social: living situation, children/dependants, domestic abuse, substance use.
