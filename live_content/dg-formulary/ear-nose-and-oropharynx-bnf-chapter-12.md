# Ear, nose and oropharynx - BNF Chapter 12

Source: https://www.rightdecisions.scot.nhs.uk/dgrefhelp-nhs-dumfries-galloway/dg-formulary/ear-nose-and-oropharynx-bnf-chapter-12/
Fetched: 2026-02-23

## 1. Otitis externa

https://cks.nice.org.uk/topics/otitis-externa/

For mild case:

- Acetic acid 2% (Earcalm) can be purchased over the counter

If red and swollen with minimal discharge:

- Lower potency steroid: Prednisolone sodium phosphate 0.5% drops
- Higher potency steroid: Betamethasone sodium phosphate 0.1% drops

If discharge/debris prevents product application, consider cleaning the ear canal first.

- Combined steroid + anti-infective product:

Betnesol N
- Otomize

For fungal infections:

- Clotrimazole 1% solution



Investigations are rarely helpful but consider an ear swab if treatment fails or otitis externa occurs frequently

## 2. Removal of ear wax

- Olive oil drops
- Sodium bicarbonate ear drops

## 2b. Nasal staphylococcal infection

- Naseptin cream (contains peanut oil)
- Fucidin Cream

For MRSA decolonisation regimes see beacon

Nasal decolonisation of MRSA:

- Mupirocin nasal (Bactroban nasal)

## 3. Nasal inflammation

- Beclomethasone dipropionate 50micrograms per 1 dose nasal spray
- Mometasone furoate 50micrograms per 1 dose nasal spray

## 4. Oropharynx

https://bnf.nice.org.uk/treatment-summary/treatment-of-dry-mouth.html

- Xerotin Oral spray
- Biotene oralbalance Gel
- Salivix Pastilles

- Chlorhexidine mouthwash
- Hydrogen peroxide Mouthwash BP (Vincent’s infection)

- Saline mouthwash made with warm water for mild cases
- Benzydamine oral rinse
- Benzydamine oromucosal spray +/- Chlorhexidine mouthwash (for secondary bacterial infections)
- Hydrocortisone muco-adhesive buccal tablets
- Beclomethasone 500microgram soluble tablets as a mouthwash
- Beclomethasone inhaler (unlicensed use – apply directly to the lesion)

See antimicrobial guidelines and

BNF https://bnf.nice.org.uk/treatment-summary/oropharyngeal-infections-antibacterial-therapy.html



- Nystatin Suspension
- Miconazole oromucosal gel
- Fluconazole capsules when topical not sutable or lesions non-reponsive to topical treatment
