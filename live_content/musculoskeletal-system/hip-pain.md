# Hip pain

Source: https://www.rightdecisions.scot.nhs.uk/dgrefhelp-nhs-dumfries-galloway/musculoskeletal-system/hip-pain/
Fetched: 2026-02-23

## Primary care management

- Assess to rule out serious pathology or red flags.
- Encourage weight loss if overweight - refer to weight management service if BMI > 40
- Encourage self management via NHS inform
- Analgesia as required
- Signpost to Single Access Point for self referral to physio

If ongoing symptoms

- refer for x-ray - pelvis AP with marker (if under 50 also request lateral hip

## Refer

Consult orthopaedic on-call or Emergency Department:

- Trauma
- Rapid deterioration
- Suspected cancer
- Previous surgery



Urgent orthopaedic outpatient referral:

- Avascular necrosis
- >50 with severe symptoms



Refer to MSK physio via SCI-gateway

- for ongoing OA symptoms. No need to dual refer to orthopaedics.
- Trochanteric bursitis
- Gait disorders/mobility assessment (not falls)
- Instability
- Labral tear
- Connective tissue disorders
