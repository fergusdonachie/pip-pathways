# Acute knee pain

Source: https://www.rightdecisions.scot.nhs.uk/dgrefhelp-nhs-dumfries-galloway/musculoskeletal-system/knee/acute-knee-pain/
Fetched: 2026-02-23

## Hot swollen joint


#### Infection suspected or recent joint surgery

Refer to Emergency department or contact on-call orthopaedic registrar

 


#### Inflammatory disease suspected

Manage in Primary Care or refer to Rheumatology

## Knee injury

Refer to ED:

- Significant Injury with rapid swelling, instability and unable to complete function
- Suspected Fracture/Extensor Mechanism Failure
- True Locked Knee

Refer direct for MRI:

- >6 weeks with  uninterrupted symptoms and
- positive meniscal provocation tests or ligament insufficiency +- joint line tenderness and
- under 50

Knee injury with ability to carry out ADLs

- Signpost to NHS Inform for gentle exercise
- Encourage functional activity
- Analgesia as required
- Refer to MSK services if progress not as expected
