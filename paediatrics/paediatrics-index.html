Evidence summary (what this index is for)

Key design points
  • In primary care, the highest-yield paediatric safety work is: recognise the sick child early, capture complete observations, and use a structured risk tool where available (e.g., traffic-light for fever under 5s).
  • NICE NG143 provides a pragmatic approach to fever in under 5s using a traffic light system (green/amber/red) + age/temperature thresholds, plus explicit guidance for remote assessment and non-paediatric practitioners.
  • Sepsis guidance for children has been separated into NICE NG254 (suspected sepsis in under‑16s); fever pathways should always include “Could this be sepsis?” prompts and red‑flag escalation.

Current included drafts

  • Fever in under 5s (no obvious source) — assess risk (traffic light), decide urgency of face-to-face review, and give safety-net advice.

Paediatrics — specialty index (primary care)

Use this page when

  • You want quick links to paediatric pathways and a reminder of the referral‑letter minimum data (especially observations + hydration/feeding).

Pathways

  • Fever in under 5s (no obvious source): assessment + initial management

General referral letter / triage metrics (paediatrics)

  • Age (exact), weight (if known), gestational age if infant, immunisation status (esp. <3 months), significant PMH (e.g., congenital heart disease, immunosuppression).
  • Time course: onset, max temp, antipyretics given (drug + time), oral intake/feeding, urine output (wet nappies), vomiting/diarrhoea, rash.
  • Obs (ideally): Temp, HR, RR, cap refill, SpO2 in air, level of alertness/interaction.
  • Exam highlights: work of breathing, hydration (mucous membranes/skin turgor), fontanelle, non‑blanching rash, neck stiffness, limb/joint use.
  • Tests already done: urine dip/culture (where appropriate), glucose (if altered conscious level), COVID/flu/RSV tests if used locally.
  • Safeguarding + social: ability to monitor at home, parental concern, transport, language barriers.
