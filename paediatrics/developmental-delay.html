Developmental Delay

Developmental delay refers to when a child does not reach developmental milestones at the expected times. These milestones can include motor skills, speech and language, cognitive skills, social and emotional development, and adaptive or daily living skills.

Referral Criteria

Consider referral to paediatric services for children presenting with:

  • Significant delay in two or more areas of development.
  • Evidence of regression in previously acquired skills.

Assessment

Assessment in primary care should include:

  • A thorough history and physical examination.
  • Assessment of growth parameters (height, weight, head circumference).
  • Hearing and vision screening.

Differential Diagnosis

Consider alternative diagnoses such as:

  • Intellectual disability
  • Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Behavioural problems
