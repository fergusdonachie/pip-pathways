Evidence summary (triangulated)

Key decision points to build the page around
  • Definition (NICE NG75): Weight fall crossing down through 2 major centile spaces, or weight below the 2nd centile (assess context).
  • Primary care role: Detailed feeding history, observation of feeds, and excluding organic disease or safeguarding concerns.
  • Management: Most cases are non-organic and respond to feeding advice and monitoring. Dietitian input is key for nutritional adequacy.

1) Recognition & Assessment

  • Measurement: Plot on UK-WHO growth charts. Use clinical judgement for "catch-down" growth in early infancy.
  • History: Focus on intake (volume/frequency/behaviour), stools, vomiting, and developmental milestones.
  • Red flags: Severe dehydration, signs of abuse/neglect (safeguarding), or signs of underlying organic disease (cardiac/respiratory/neuro).

2) Management strategy

  • Feeding support: Optimize position, latch, and environment. Address parental anxiety.
  • Monitoring: Weigh at appropriate intervals (e.g., weekly/fortnightly) to avoid anxiety-driven daily weighing.
  • Referral: Required if persistent despite primary care/health visitor intervention, or if organic cause is suspected.

  • Dietitian Access: Confirm if GPs can refer directly to community dietitians for faltering growth without paediatric review first.
  • Health Visitor Role: Clarify local thresholds for Health Visitor-led monitoring vs GP review.

Failure to thrive (Faltering Growth)

Background

  • Definition: A weight fall crossing down through 2 major centile spaces, or weight below the 2nd centile (assess context).
  • Most cases are non-organic and relate to feeding difficulties or psychosocial factors.
  • Measurement should be plotted on UK-WHO growth charts.

Assessment

  • History: Detailed feeding history (frequency, volume, behaviour), vomiting/stooling, developmental milestones, family history.
  • Examination: Dysmorphism, signs of organic disease (cardiac, respiratory, neuro), hydration status, signs of neglect/abuse.
  • Observation: Observe a feed if possible (latch, swallow, parent-child interaction).
  • Investigations: Usually not indicated initially unless organic cause suspected (e.g. coeliac screen, urine culture).

Primary Care Management

  • Feeding support: Optimize position, frequency, and content. Address parental anxiety.
  • Monitoring: Weigh at appropriate intervals (e.g., weekly/fortnightly, not daily) to monitor trends.
  • Health Visitor: Liaise closely for community support and weight monitoring.
  • Dietitian: Consider early referral if nutritional intake is inadequate.

Who to refer

Emergency / Same-day (Admit)

  • Severe dehydration.
  • Safeguarding concerns (immediate action).
  • Signs of serious underlying illness (e.g. cardiac failure).

Routine Paediatrics

  • Persistent faltering despite primary care/HV interventions.
  • Suspected underlying organic cause (e.g. GORD, allergy, coeliac).
  • Significant parental anxiety or inability to cope.

Who not to refer

  • Initial presentation with no red flags (trial feeding support first).
  • Normal physiological "catch-down" growth (e.g. large birth weight moving to genetic potential).
