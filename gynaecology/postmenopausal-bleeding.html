Evidence summary (triangulated)

Related pages
  • Bleeding on HRT (unscheduled bleeding in people using HRT).
  • Abnormal uterine bleeding (if not clearly postmenopausal).
  • Postcoital bleeding (if bleeding only after sex).
  • Symptoms suggestive of cervical cancer (PCB/discharge) — referral & colposcopy (if cervix suspicious / symptoms suggest cervical cancer).
Key design decisions for the D&G page
  • D&G local process: PMB is managed via a parallel referral to TVUS and to gynaecology for clinic review regardless of ultrasound threshold (no explicit endometrial thickness cutoff stated in the local pathway).
  • East vs West operational difference (at GP end):
    • West: arrange TVUS first (as part of parallel referral) and patient is reviewed in clinic.
    • East: refer to the clinic; gynaecology has access to USS in clinic (still parallel in effect).
  • Triage destinations (secondary care): recurrent PMB and people on tamoxifen may be seen directly in hysteroscopy clinic; suspected cervical cancer may be seen directly in colposcopy clinic. This depends on referral information.

Comparator pathways (what differs elsewhere)

  • Many UK pathways use a TVUS endometrial thickness threshold to decide referral urgency (commonly 5 mm in some services), and may discharge with safety‑netting if ET is below threshold and no focal lesion.
  • Example comparator (RefHelp Lothian): states that with PMB, normal exam, and endometrial thickness <5.0 mm (and no focal pathology), this may be managed without onward referral; if ET ≥5.0 mm, onward referral to gynaecology is required. This is presented here purely to highlight that D&G appears to have chosen a review‑regardless model rather than a strict ET gate.
Implication for the D&G DGRefHelp page
  • Make the local process explicit: parallel TVUS + gynae referral (no threshold gate) and highlight which clinic may receive the patient (general / hysteroscopy / colposcopy) depending on referral details.
  • Because triage tools/scores may be applied in secondary care, the DGRefHelp page should state the metrics the triaging team needs in the referral letter (see “Who to refer”).
If you want, we can add a short “Why D&G differs” paragraph once you confirm whether capacity/clinic model is the driver (e.g., one‑stop clinic vs radiology triage).

Postmenopausal bleeding

Related pages
  • Bleeding on HRT
  • Abnormal uterine bleeding
  • Postcoital bleeding
  • Symptoms suggestive of cervical cancer (PCB/discharge) — referral & colposcopy

Background

  • Postmenopausal bleeding (PMB) = bleeding >12 months after last menstrual period (or any bleeding in someone who is established postmenopausal).
  • Main priority is to exclude malignancy (endometrial and cervical) while recognising common benign causes (atrophy, polyps, HRT-related bleeding).
  • D&G local process: parallel referral model — TVUS plus gynaecology review (no stated endometrial thickness threshold gate).

Assessment (primary care) — collapsible

  • History: onset, amount, recurrence/persistence; pain; discharge; postcoital bleeding; weight loss; anticoagulants; HRT regimen and timing; tamoxifen; cervical screening history (incl last test/result).
  • Examination: abdominal and speculum exam where appropriate; look for cervical lesion; bimanual if indicated.
  • Red flags / urgent same day: haemodynamic compromise, severe pain, sepsis features, heavy ongoing bleeding not controlled.

Primary care management

  • Do not wait for symptoms to settle: arrange referral per local pathway.
  • D&G local process: make a parallel referral for TVUS and gynaecology assessment (patient reviewed regardless of ultrasound threshold).
  • If there is concern for cervical malignancy on speculum exam, make this explicit to enable colposcopy triage.

Who to refer

Key principle: the referral letter content affects triage (general clinic vs hysteroscopy clinic vs colposcopy clinic). Include the metrics secondary care needs.

All PMB (local pathway)

  • Refer all PMB via the local pathway, using the parallel referral model (TVUS + gynaecology review).

East vs West (operational)

  • West: arrange TVUS first (as part of parallel referral) then clinic review.
  • East: refer to the clinic; gynaecology has access to USS in clinic.

May be triaged directly (secondary care)

  • Recurrent PMB and/or on tamoxifen: may be seen directly in hysteroscopy clinic (same GP-end pathway; depends on referral detail).
  • Suspicious cervical cancer (abnormal cervix/visible lesion): may be seen directly in colposcopy clinic (triage depends on referral detail).

Referral letter: required information (triage metrics)

  • Bleeding pattern: single episode vs persistent vs recurrent (dates, frequency).
  • HRT: none / sequential / continuous combined; any missed progestogen; any unscheduled bleeding context.
  • Tamoxifen use (current/past).
  • Speculum findings: normal vs visible cervical lesion / contact bleeding / suspicious features.
  • Cervical screening history (last test date/result; whether up to date).
  • Any anticoagulation and relevant comorbidities.
  • If available by the time of referral: TVUS outcome (endometrial thickness, focal lesion/polyp, adnexal findings).

Who not to refer

  • In D&G, do not use an endometrial thickness threshold to avoid referral: the local model is parallel TVUS + review.
