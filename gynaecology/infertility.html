Evidence summary (triangulated)

Key points for a GP-facing pathway page
  • Infertility affects both partners; NICE advises seeing the couple together when possible.
  • Timing advice: vaginal intercourse every 2–3 days optimises chance of pregnancy.
  • Age matters: fertility declines with age; referral urgency commonly increases with age and duration of trying (local criteria may apply).
  • Baseline workup should include key information for triage: duration trying, menstrual pattern/ovulation evidence, BMI, smoking, past pelvic infection/surgery, semen analysis, and basic endocrine tests where indicated.

NICE CG156 (fertility problems) – core principles

  • Encourages accessible information, counselling support, and specialist team-based care.
  • Includes lifestyle factors (BMI, smoking) and optimising natural conception chances.
Local (D&G)
  • Service: Fertility clinic under Gynae.
  • Semen analysis is required for referral/triage.
  • Eligibility/thresholds for referral and assisted conception are often region-specific (age/BMI/duration criteria) — keep these as local agreement items.

Infertility

Background

  • Infertility is usually defined as failure to conceive after 12 months of regular unprotected intercourse; earlier referral may be appropriate based on age or known risk factors.
  • Both partners should be considered; a baseline semen analysis is usually required for triage.

Assessment (primary care) — collapsible

  • History (both partners): duration trying; timing/frequency of intercourse; prior pregnancies; contraception history; STI/PID; pelvic/abdominal surgery; endometriosis; menstrual pattern/ovulation clues; dyspareunia; medications; smoking/alcohol; BMI.
  • Examination: BMI; consider pelvic exam if symptoms suggest fibroids/endometriosis or pelvic pathology.
  • Baseline tests: semen analysis; confirm ovulation (e.g., mid-luteal progesterone if regular cycles); thyroid/prolactin if indicated; STI screening if risk.

Primary care management

  • Advise intercourse every 2–3 days; consider timed intercourse around ovulation if desired.
  • Optimise modifiable factors: smoking cessation; weight optimisation (BMI high or low); review medications; folic acid for women trying to conceive.
  • Treat identified reversible causes where possible while arranging referral as appropriate.

Who to refer

Include triage metrics. Fertility services often cannot triage without semen analysis + ovulation evidence + duration trying.

  • Early referral if known risk factors (very irregular/absent periods; suspected severe endometriosis; history of PID; previous pelvic surgery; known male factor; age-related urgency).
  • Routine referral after 12 months of trying to conceive (or per local criteria).

Referral letter: required information (triage metrics)

  • Both partners’ details and ages; duration trying; prior pregnancies (either partner).
  • Menstrual pattern; ovulation evidence (progesterone result if done).
  • BMI, smoking status, alcohol; relevant comorbidities and medications.
  • History of PID/STIs; endometriosis symptoms; pelvic surgery.
  • Semen analysis report (date + result) if performed.
  • Any relevant tests: TSH/prolactin if done; STI screening if done.
  • What the couple want: natural conception support vs assisted conception; any safeguarding/psychosocial needs.

Who not to refer

  • Couples trying for less than 12 months with no risk factors may be managed with advice and follow-up (confirm local criteria).
