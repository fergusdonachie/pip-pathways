## Fibroids Referral Pathway ### Assessment * History: Detailed menstrual history, pain assessment, impact on quality of life. * Examination: Abdominal and pelvic examination to assess uterine size and tenderness. ### Indications for Referral * Symptomatic fibroids causing significant: * Heavy menstrual bleeding (HMB) unresponsive to first-line treatments. * Pelvic pain affecting daily activities. * Pressure symptoms (e.g., urinary frequency, constipation). * Uncertain diagnosis or suspicion of malignancy. * Fibroids causing subfertility or recurrent pregnancy loss. * Large fibroids (>10 cm) or rapidly growing fibroids. ### Referral Pathway 1. **Primary Care Management:** * Initial assessment and investigations as outlined above. * Trial of first-line medical treatments (e.g., NSAIDs, tranexamic acid, hormonal therapies). 2. **Secondary Care Referral:** * Refer to Gynaecology clinic for further evaluation and management. * Include relevant history, examination findings, and investigation results in the referral. 3. **Secondary Care Management:** * Further investigations (e.g., ultrasound, hysteroscopy) as indicated. * Discussion of management options based on individual patient factors and preferences. * Consideration of medical, surgical, or interventional treatments. ### Information to Include in Referral * Patient demographics and contact information. * Detailed menstrual history (including HMB assessment). * Pain assessment (including severity and impact on quality of life). * Examination findings (including uterine size and tenderness). * Results of relevant investigations (e.g., ultrasound). * Previous treatments and response. * Patientâ€™s goals and preferences. ### Resources * NICE guidelines on the management of fibroids. **Local agreement needed:** Local pathway variations may apply.
